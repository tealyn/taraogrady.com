About me

Hello! I am a Human-Computer Interaction MA candidate at the State University of New York at Oswego. I completed my undergraduate degree in Cognitive Science and have a strong interests in UX research, UX design, Virtual Reality, Augmented Reality, Affective Computing, and Data Visualization.

I am an advocate for closing the gender gap in technology, and am an active member of Girls Who Code. Annie Reynolds and I started the Oswego Girls Who Code Chapter in 2017 and have been active facilitators since.

While in Vietnam this past summer Annie Reynolds and I were asked to host a fireside chat at SURF 2018, an international startup conference on the topic "Empowering Girls Through Coding."

Additionally, we presented at Google I/O Extended Mien Trung on the topic "Augmented Reality and its applications to educational technology" in collaboration with Jolanda Tromp.
